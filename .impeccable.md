## Design Context

### Users
People (initially: a tired mom) who open the app in the evening — typically after a long, draining day — looking for a low-effort way to decompress and reflect. They're on mobile, probably on the couch or in bed, in a dimly lit room. They're not looking for productivity tools or dashboards. They want to feel held, not managed. The core job: show up, check in, let go a little.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: still, deep, unhurried.**

The emotional goal is the feeling of running a hot bath after an exhausting day — candles lit, soft music on, nowhere to be. The app should feel like that bath: warm, sensory, slow. Nothing demands attention. Everything waits for you.

Voice is quiet and intimate. Like a journal that writes back gently. Not a wellness coach, not a therapist — more like a trusted notebook with warm lighting.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme: Dark.** Not tech-dark (no cyan glows, no purple-to-blue gradients, no neon). Warm dark — the colour of a room lit only by candles. Deep indigo-brown backgrounds, not cold navy.
- **Palette anchor:** Candlelight amber/terracotta as the accent. Warm cream text. Very dark, slightly warm-tinted backgrounds. Gold used sparingly as a secondary warmth.
- **Typography:** Graceful — not flashy. A warm serif with quiet character for headings/prompts. A clean, slightly soft humanist sans for body and labels. Not "editorial magazine" serif — more "journal cover on a nightstand."
- **Density: Low.** One thought per screen. Generous breathing room. Never crowd the user. Minimalism here is about *quiet*, not *sterility*.
- **Anti-references:** Fitness trackers, productivity apps, mental-health clinic portals, anything with a dashboard. Also avoid: startup wellness app aesthetic (lime green + white + icons), generic "zen" app (white + pastel + lotus emoji), and dark-mode tech tools (neon on black).

### Design Principles
1. **One breath at a time.** Each screen holds one idea. Never compete for attention. The user is already overwhelmed — the interface should feel like relief, not another inbox.
2. **Warmth over polish.** A touch of imperfection feels human. Prefer warmth and ease over surgical precision. Rounded, unhurried, never sharp or clinical.
3. **Ambient, not alert.** Motion should be slow and soft — fade, dissolve, drift. Nothing snaps, bounces, or demands. Transitions should feel like turning a page.
4. **The dark is cozy, not dramatic.** Dark backgrounds should feel like a candlelit room, not a cockpit. Use warm-tinted darks, not pure black or cold navy.
5. **Mobile is the only screen that matters.** Every design decision starts from a phone held in one hand, in dim light, with tired eyes.
